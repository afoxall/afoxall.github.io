Queen's University Research Assistant

May 2015-present

I spent this summer working at the Reactor Materials Testing Laboratory, a nuclear materials research facility run by the Queen's Department of Mechanical and Materials Engineering. RMTL houses a proton accelerator used to study the effects of radiation on the mechanical properties of materials.

Software & Electrical

My main job is to set up temperature controls and data acquisition for a new sample holder. I have written Labview code to, among other things, handle serial communication with two PID controllers, measure current through an Arduino, and control a thermocouple multiplexer. I also designed and populated a PCB for the op amp circuits which allow the Arduino to measure currents and did most of the electrical connections for the sample holder.

Design Project

Besides my interfacing work I also designed a new apparatus: an array of faraday cups used to image a cross-section of the proton beam. This project presents lots of new challenges from accurately measuring nanocurrents to the complexities of ultra-high vacuum design. I will continue working at RMTL part time all year, building the apparatus I designed and doing interfacing for a new tensile rig.

The best part of this job is how much I'm learning. I have very little supervision, and having just finished my first year of engineering I didn't know much going in. I've had to teach myself everything from Labview itself to serial communications to soldering circuits. It doesn't get much better than getting paid to learn!
