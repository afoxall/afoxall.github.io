Queen's University Research Assistant

I spent this summer working at the Reactor Materials Testing Laboratory, a nuclear materials research facility run by the Queen's Department of Mechanical and Materials Engineering. RMTL houses a proton accelerator used to study the effects of radiation on the mechanical properties of materials.

My main job is to set up temperature controls and data acquisition for a new sample holder. So far I have written Labview code to handle serial communication with two PID controllers, and current detection through an Arduino. I also designed a PCB that holds the op amp circuits which allow the Arduino to measure currents.

The best part of this job is how much I'm learning. I have very little supervision, and having just finished my first year of engineering I didn't know much going in. I've had to teach myself everything from Labview itself to serial communications to soldering circuits. It doesn't get much better than getting paid to learn!

Besides my interfacing work I also designed a new apparatus: an array of faraday cups used to image a cross-section of the proton beam. This project presents lots of new challenges from accurately measuring nanocurrents to the complexities of ultra-high vacuum design. I will continue working at RMTL part time all year, building the apparatus I designed and doing interfacing to a new tensile rig.
